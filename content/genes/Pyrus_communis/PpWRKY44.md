## Functional Description:
   - Taken together, these findings suggest that **PpWRKY44** and PpABF3 play positive roles in salinity-induced malate accumulation in pears.
   - Light-responsive transcription factor **PpWRKY44** induces anthocyanin accumulation by regulating PpMYB10 expression in pear.
   - Functional analysis based on overexpressed pear calli showed that PpWRKY44 promoted anthocyanin accumulation.
   - Transiently overexpressed **PpWRKY44** in pear leaves and fruit peels significantly enhanced the accumulation of anthocyanin, whereas silencing **PpWRKY44** in pear fruit peels impaired induction of the accumulation of anthocyanin by light.
   - Our results explained the mechanism mediating the impacts of **PpWRKY44** on the transcriptional regulation of anthocyanin accumulation, with potential implications for fine-tuning the fruit peel coloration triggered by light in red pears.
