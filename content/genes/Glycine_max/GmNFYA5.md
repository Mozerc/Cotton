## Functional Descriptions:
   - Our results suggested that overexpression of **GmNFYA5** improved drought tolerance in soybean via both ABA-dependent and ABA-independent pathways.
   - Overexpression of **GmNFYA5** confers drought tolerance to transgenic Arabidopsis and soybean plants.
   - Soybean NF-Y gene **GmNFYA5** was identified to have the highest transcript level among all 21 NF-YA genes in soybean (Glycine max L.) under drought stress.
   - **GmNFYA5** was suppressed by the ABA synthesis inhibitor naproxen (NAP).
   - **GmNFYA5** probably regulated the transcript abundance of GmDREB2 and GmbZIP1 by binding to the promoters in vivo.
   - **GmNFYA5** should be a positive gene that can increase drought resistance and therefore has potential for use in molecular breeding of soybean.
