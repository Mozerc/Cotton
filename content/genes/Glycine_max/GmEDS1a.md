## Functional Descriptions:
   - Silencing of **GmEDS1a**/b and GmPAD4 stopped accumulation of salicylic acid and increased the plant's suceptibility to Pseudomonas syringae pv glycinea and soybean mosaic virus infection.
   - GmEDS1 and GmPAD4 Contribute to Defense Responses in Soybean.
   - We identified two EDS1-like (**GmEDS1a**/GmEDS1b) proteins and one PAD4-like (GmPAD4) protein that are required for resistance signaling in soybean.
   - The **GmEDS1a**/GmEDS1b and GmPAD4 genes were also required for Resistance to Pseudomonas syringae pv glycinea2 (Rpg2)-mediated resistance to Pseudomonas syringae.
   - GmEDS1 and GmPAD4 Contribute to Defense Responses in Soybean.
