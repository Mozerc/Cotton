## Functional Descriptions:
   - Natural variation of **GmFATA1B** regulates seed oil content and composition in soybean.
   - Identified **GmFATA1B** as the gene underlying a QTL that regulates seed oil content and composition, as well as seed size in soybean.
   - Nine extra amino acids in the conserved region of **GmFATA1B** impair its function as a fatty acyl–acyl carrier protein thioesterase, thereby affecting seed oil content and composition.
   - Heterogeneously overexpressing the functional **GmFATA1B** allele in Arabidopsis thaliana increased both the total oil content and the oleic acid and linoleic acid contents of seeds. Our findings uncover a previously unknown locus underlying variation in seed oil content in soybean and lay the foundation for improving seed oil content and composition in soybean.
