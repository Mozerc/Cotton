## Functional Descriptions:
   - The apple (Malus × domestica) FRK gene **MdFRK2** shows especially high affinity to fructose, and its overexpression decreases fructose levels in the leaves of young plants.
   - In the current study of mature plants, fruits of transgenic apple trees overexpressing **MdFRK2** accumulated a higher level of fructose than wild-type (WT) fruits (at both young and mature stages).
   - Transgenic apple trees with high mRNA **MdFRK2** expression showed no significant differences in **MdFRK2** protein abundance or FRK enzyme activity compared to WT in mature leaves, young fruits, and mature fruits.
   - MdCacyBP–**MdFRK2** regulatory model is crucial for regulating fructose accumulation.
