## Functional Descriptions:
   - **OsCBM1** physically interacts with OsRbohA, a NADPH oxidase, and the expression of **OsCBM1** in osrbohA, an OsRbohA-knockout mutant, is significantly downregulated under both normal growth and drought stress conditions.
   - Overexpression of **OsCBM1** increased drought tolerance and reactive oxygen species production in rice, whereas the knockdown of the gene decreased them.
   - All these results suggest that **OsCBM1** participates in NADPH oxidase-mediated ROS production by interacting with OsRbohA and OsRacGEF1, contributing to drought stress tolerance of rice.
   - **OsCBM1** is localized in both the endoplasmic reticulum and plasma membrane.
