## Functional Descriptions:
   - Xenopus laevis oocytes expressing **TOM1** or Hv**TOM1** released (14)C-labeled deoxymugineic acid but not (14)C-labeled nicotianamine, a structural analog and biosynthetic precursor of deoxymugineic acid, indicating that the **TOM1** and Hv**TOM1** proteins are the phytosiderophore efflux transporters.
   - In rice roots, the efficiency of deoxymugineic acid secretion was enhanced by overexpression of **TOM1** and decreased by its repression, providing further evidence that **TOM1** encodes the efflux transporter of deoxymugineic acid.
   - Under conditions of iron deficiency, rice and barley roots express high levels of **TOM1** and Hv**TOM1**, respectively, and the overexpression of these genes increased tolerance to iron deficiency.
   - Transcripts of **OsZIFL4**, OsZIFL5, OsZIFL7, and OsZIFL12 accumulate in response to Zn-excess and Fe-deficiency in roots, two stresses with partially overlapping responses.
