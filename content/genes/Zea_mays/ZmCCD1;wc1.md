## Functional Descriptions:
   - Mycorrhization led to a decrease in seed germination of the parasitic plant Striga hermonthica as examined in a bioassay. **ZmCCD1** is proposed to be involved in cyclohexenone and mycorradicin formation in mycorrhizal maize roots but not in strigolactone formation.
   - Dominant **Wc1** alleles enhance he white-grain phenotype of y1.
   - Dominant **Wc1** kernel with white crown and pale yellow endosperm.
   - In maize, roots with mycorrhizal formations have shown a higher **ZmCCD1** expression and induced lower germination of Striga.
   - Since the **ZmCCD1** gene is involved in the formation of the yellow pigment apocarotenoids, it would be interesting in the future to understand the carotenoid levels of the lines and its relationship with Striga resistance in the maize inbred lines.
