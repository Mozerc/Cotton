## Functional Descriptions:
   - The roothairless1 (**RTH1**) mutant is impaired in root hair elongation and exhibits other growth abnormalities.
   - The cloning of the **RTH1** gene associates the homologs of exocyst subunits to an exocytotic process in plant development and supports the hypothesis that exocyst-like proteins are involved in plant exocytosis.
   - **RTH1** gene, which is instrumental in the exocytotic tip growth of root hairs.
   - The preferential accumulation in the **RTH1** mutant proteome of a negative regulator of the cell cycle (a prohibitin) may at least partially explain the delayed development and flowering of the **RTH1** mutant.
