## Functional Descriptions:
   - ZmPTF1, a phosphate starvation-induced basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor, contributes to root development and low-phosphate tolerance in maize.
   - The improved root system, increased ABA content, and activated ABA-, CBF4-, ATAF2-, and NAC30-mediated stress responses increased the drought tolerance of the ZmPTF1 overexpression lines, while the mutants showed opposite trends.
   - **ZmbZIP4** Contributes to Stress Resistance in Maize by Regulating ABA Synthesis and Root Development.
   - **ZmbZIP4** is a positive regulator of plant abiotic stress responses and is involved in root development in maize.
   - **ZmbZIP4** is involved in maize root growth.
