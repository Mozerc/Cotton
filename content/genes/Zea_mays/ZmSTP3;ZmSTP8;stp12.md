## Functional Descriptions:
   - Extracellular sucrose is hydrolyzed to monosaccharides by cell wall acid invertase and subsequently absorbed as monosaccharides by the monosaccharide transporters **ZmSTP3** and Zmsweet31/4C.
   - **ZmSTP3** likely exporting sucrose but enhanced the expression of most transporter genes for uptake of apoplasmic sugars.
   - The presence of ZmSWEET11 and 13b (Clade III) in the maternal tissue (facilitating sucrose efflux to the apoplasm) and **ZmSTP3** and ZmSWEET4c (Clade II) in the BETL (for the uptake of hexoses) indicated that sucrose is likely degraded at the maternal–filial interface.
