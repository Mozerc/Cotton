## Functional Descriptions:
   - The maize **P1** gene encodes an R2R3-MYB transcription factor that controls the biosynthesis of red flavonoid pigments in floral tissues of the maize plant.
   - Genetic and quantitative trait locus analyses have also associated the **P1** gene with the synthesis of maysin, a flavone glycoside from maize silks that confers natural resistance to corn earworm.
   - We show directly that the **P1** gene induces maysin accumulation in silk tissues.
   - **P1** transcription factor directs production of silk maysin, a corn earworm resistance factor, in concordance with a hierarchy of floral organ pigmentation.
   - The **P1** transgenes also conferred red pigment to pericarp, cob, husk and tassel tissues.
   - Transformation with a **P1** transgene induces maysin accumulation in maize silks.
