## Functional Descriptions:
   - The high expression levels of **ZmBRI1a** and zmbri1b, but not the other brl genes, suggest that the BRI1 receptors encoded by these two genes are of primary importance for regulating leaf growth.
   - **ZmBRI1a** and Zmbri1b expression was high at the base of the leaf in the zones of active cell division and cell elongation, gradually declining as cells differentiate and mature.
   - **ZmBRI1a** encodes a brassinosteroid receptor.
   - Autophagy receptor ZmNBR1 promotes the autophagic degradation of **ZmBRI1a** and enhances drought tolerance in maize.
   - Determined that BRASSINOSTEROID INSENSITIVE 1A (**ZmBRI1a**), a brassinosteroid receptor of the BRI1-like family, interacts with ZmNBR1.
   - ZmNBR1 regulates the expression of autophagy-related genes, thereby increasing autophagic activity and promoting the autophagic degradation of **ZmBRI1a** under drought stress, thus enhancing drought tolerance in maize.
