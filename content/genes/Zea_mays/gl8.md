## Functional Descriptions:
   - The **Gl8** gene is required for the normal accumulation of cuticular waxes on maize (Zea mays) seedling leaves.
   - The predicted **Gl8** protein exhibits significant sequence similarity to a class of enzymes that catalyze the reduction of a ketone group to a hydroxyl group.
   - The Endoplasmic Reticulum-Associated Maize **Gl8** Protein Is a Component of the Acyl-Coenzyme A Elongase Involved in the Production of Cuticular Waxes.
   - **Gl8** protein functions as a Î²-ketoacyl reductase during the elongation of very long-chain fatty acids required for the production of cuticular waxes.
   - In combination, these findings indicate that the **Gl8** protein is a component of the acyl-CoA elongase.
