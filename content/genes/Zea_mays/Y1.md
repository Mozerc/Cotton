## Functional Descriptions:
   - More recently, breeders have selected yellow endosperm variants of maize over ancestral white phenotypes for their increased nutritional value resulting from the up-regulation of the **Y1** phytoene synthase gene product in endosperm tissue.
   - **Y1** a key gene controlling carotenoid pigment in endosperm which was proved under breeding selection.
   - The maize **Y1** gene on chromosome 6 has undergone recent selection for endosperm color phenotype.
   - Long-range patterns of diversity and linkage disequilibrium surrounding the maize **Y1** gene are indicative of an asymmetric selective sweep.
