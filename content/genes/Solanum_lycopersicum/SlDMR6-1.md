## Functional Descriptions:
   - We show that **SlDMR6-1**, but not SlDMR6-2, is associated with plant immunity. Remarkably, **SlDMR6-1** mutants display enhanced resistance to bacterial, oomycete, and fungal pathogens.
   - We also demonstrate that **SlDMR6-1** and SlDMR6-2 convert SA into its inactive form, 2,5-DHBA, indicating that they play a role in SA homeostasis.
   - **SlDMR6-1** mutants display enhanced resistance against different classes of pathogens, such as bacteria, oomycete, and fungi.
   - We then propose that SlDMR6 duplication in tomato resulted in subsequent subfunctionalization, in which SlDMR6-2 specialized in balancing SA levels in flowers/fruits, while **SlDMR6-1** conserved the ability to fine-tune SA levels during pathogen infection of the plant vegetative tissues.
