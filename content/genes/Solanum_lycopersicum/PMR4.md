## Functional Descriptions:
   - Previously, RNAi silencing of the susceptibility (S) gene PowderyMildewResistance 4 (**PMR4**) in tomato has been shown to enhance resistance against the powdery mildew pathogen Oidium neolycopersici (On).
   - CRISPR/Cas9-targeted mutagenesis of the tomato susceptibility gene **PMR4** for resistance against powdery mildew.
   - To study whether full knock-out of the tomato **PMR4** gene would result in a higher level of resistance than in the RNAi-silenced transgenic plants we generated tomato **PMR4** CRISPR mutants.
   - Resistance to powdery mildew in sl**PMR4** mutants.
   - In Arabidopsis it was shown that PM resistance in the **PMR4** mutant is associated with an activation of the SA signal transduction pathway.
