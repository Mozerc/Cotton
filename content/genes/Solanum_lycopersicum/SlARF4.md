## Functional Descriptions:
   - The auxin response factor gene **SlARF4**, which was highly expressed in II, V and VI type trichomes, positively regulated the auxin-induced formation of II, V and VI type trichomes in the tomato leaves.
   - **SlARF4** overexpression plants with high densities of these trichomes exhibited tolerance to spider mites. Two R2R3 MYB genes, SlTHM1 and SlMYB52, were directly targeted and inhibited by **SlARF4**.
   - **SlARF4** is a repressor of auxin response and regulates the accumulation of chlorophyll and starch in tomato fruits.
   - The regulation of **SlARF4** on the formation of II, V and VI type trichomes depended on SlTHM1 and SlMYB52, which directly targeted cyclin gene SlCycB2 and increased its expression.
