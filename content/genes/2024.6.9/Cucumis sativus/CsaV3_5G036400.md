## Functional Descriptions:
   - The reverse mutation of **CsMLO8** results in susceptibility to powdery mildew via inhibiting cell wall apposition formation and cell death in cucumber.
   - The resistant line contains a transposon in susceptibility gene **CsMLO8**, causing alternative splicing and loss of function of the gene.
   - Results showed that the loss of function of **CsMLO8** mediated resistance to powdery mildew was related to cell wall apposition formation and cell death.
   - The **CsMLO8** revertant (m6326) showed a higher rate of spore penetration, longer hypha and more conidia.
   - **CsMLO8**/CsMLO11 are required for full susceptibility of cucumber stem to powdery mildew and interact with CsCRK2 and CsRbohD**CsMLO8**/11 are required for full susceptibility of cucumber stem to powdery mildew and interact with CsCRK2 and CsRbohD.
