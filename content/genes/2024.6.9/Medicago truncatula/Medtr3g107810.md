## Functional Descriptions:
   - Specific tissue proteins 1 and 6 are involved in root biology during normal development and under symbiotic and pathogenic interactions in Medicago truncatula.
   - ST1 and **ST6** are possibly involved in primary and lateral root and symbiotic nodule development, but only **ST6** participates in the interaction with hemibiotrophic fungi.
   - The role of ST1 and **ST6** in the nodulation process may be related to nodule organogenesis rather than to the establishment of the interaction itself, and an increase in **ST6** correlates with the activation of the salicylic acid signaling pathway during the infection and colonization processes. 
   - Gene expression results show that ST1 and **ST6** participate in the vasculature development of both primary and lateral roots, although only **ST6** is related to meristem activity.
