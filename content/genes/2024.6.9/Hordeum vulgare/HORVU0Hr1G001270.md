## Functional Descriptions:
   - Identified a salt-responsive calmodulin (**HvCaM1**) in a proteome study of barley (Hordeum vulgare) roots.
   - **HvCaM1** was mainly expressed in roots and was significantly up-regulated in response to long-term salt stress,**HvCaM1** is an intracellular signaling protein that localizes to the root stele and vascular systems of barley.
   - **HvCaM1** knockdown (RNA interference) lines showed significantly larger biomass but lower Na+ concentration, xylem Na+ loading, and Na+ transportation rates in shoots compared with overexpression lines and wild-type plants.
   - **HvCaM1** negatively regulates salt tolerance, probably via interaction with HvCAMTA4 to modulate the down-regulation of HvHKT1|5 and/or the up-regulation of HvHKT1|1 to reduce shoot Na+ accumulation under salt stress in barley.
