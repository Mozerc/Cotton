## Functional Descriptions:
   - E3 ubiquitin ligases MdSINA4 and **MdSINA11** regulate anthocyanin biosynthesis by targeting the IAA29-ARF5-1-ERF3 module in apple.
   - The E3 ubiquitin ligases MdSINA4 and **MdSINA11** played negative and positive regulatory roles in anthocyanin biosynthesis by targeting MdIAA29 and MdARF5-1 for ubiquitination degradation, respectively.
   - MdSINA4 destabilized **MdSINA11** to regulate anthocyanin accumulation in response to auxin signalling.
   - MdSINA4 was involved in the ubiquitination modification of **MdSINA11**, which contributes to the balanced regulation of the auxin response.
