## Functional Descriptions:
   - The transcription factor **CsS40** negatively regulates TCS1 expression and caffeine biosynthesis in connection to leaf senescence in Camellia sinensis.
   - A novel transcription factor Camellia sinensis Senescnece 40 (**CsS40**) as a caffeine biosynthesis regulator, which was discovered during screening a yeast expression library constructed from tea leaf cDNAs for activation of tea caffeine synthase (TCS1) promoter.
   - Overexpression of **CsS40** reduced the accumulation of xanthine and hypoxanthine in tobacco plants, meanwhile, increased their susceptibility to aging. **CsS40** expression in tea leaves was also induced by senescence-promoting hormones and environmental factors.
