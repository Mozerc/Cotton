## Functional Descriptions:
   - The reverse-transcription quantitative PCR (RT-qPCR) analysis showed that the **TaARF15** homoeologs were expressed constitutively in various wheat tissues but were preferentially expressed in stems and young spikes, which may be related to the transport and distribution of auxin
   - The **TaARF15** homoeologs share the same three domains including a DNA-binding domain (DBD), a middle region (MR), and a C-terminal dimerization domain (CTD), suggesting they might share similar biological functions.
   - TaARF15 homoeologs (TaARF15-A1, **TaARF15-B1**, and TaARF15-D1) in wheat. OE of TaARF15-A1 delays leaf senescence, while downregulation of **TaARF15** by RNA-interference (RNAi) or knockout of this gene by CRISPR-Cas9-mediated genome editing accelerates maturation.
   - The auxin response factor **TaARF15** negatively regulates senescence in common wheat.
   - Wheat AUXIN RESPONSE FACTOR 15 (**TaARF15**) delays senescence through interaction at the TaNAM1 locus.
