## Functional Descriptions:
   - Cucumber Phospholipase D alpha (**CsPLDα**) gene overexpression in tobacco enhanced drought stress tolerance by regulating stomatal closure and lipid peroxidation.
   - The **CsPLDα** overexpression in tobacco lines correlated with the ABA synthesis and metabolism, regulated the rapid stomatal closure in drought stress, and reduced the water loss.
   - The plants with overexpressed **CsPLDα** exhibited lower water loss, higher leaf relative water content, and heavier fresh and dry matter accumulation than the WT. We proposed that **CsPLDα** was involved in the ABA-dependent pathway in mediating the stomatal closure and preventing the elevation of intracellular solute potential.
   - We characterized the roles of the cucumber phospholipase D alpha gene (**CsPLDα**, GenBank accession number EF363796) in the growth and tolerance of transgenic tobacco (Nicotiana tabacum) to drought stress.
