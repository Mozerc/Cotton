## Functional Descriptions:
   - Identified and characterized a miR160a–**GmARF16**–GmMYC2 module and its regulation during the salt–stress response in soybean.
   - **GmARF16** activates GmMYC2, encoding a bHLH transcription factor that reduces salinity tolerance by down-regulating proline biosynthesis.
   - Demonstrate that natural variation in **GmARF16** can alleviate the detrimental effects of salt stress and its artificial selection for adaptation to salinity has geographic variation.
   - These results indicate **GmARF16**H3 has undergone artificial selection to confer adaptation to salinity, thus rendering it a promising candidate for soybean breeding to enhance salt tolerance.
