## Functional Descriptions:
   - Constitutive expression of **CaSRP1**, a hot pepper small rubber particle protein homolog, resulted in fast growth and improved drought tolerance in transgenic Arabidopsis plants.
   - The predicted **CaSRP1** protein sequence exhibited significant amino acid identity to putative stress-related proteins and the small rubber particle protein (SRPP) found in rubber trees (Hevea brasiliensis)
   - Overexpression of **CaSRP1** resulted in enhanced root and shoot growth and earlier bolting in the transgenic plants relative to wild-type plants.
   - These results suggest that **CaSRP1** plays dual functions as a positive factor for tissue growth and development and for drought-defensive responses. A possible cellular function of SRPP homologs in non-rubber-producing plants in relation to drought stress tolerance is discussed.
