## Functional Descriptions:
   - PacCYP707A2 negatively regulates cherry fruit ripening while **PacCYP707A1** mediates drought tolerance.
   - In this study, four cDNAs (**PacCYP707A1**–4) encoding 8′-hydroxylase, a key enzyme in the oxidative catabolism of ABA, were identified in sweet cherry fruits using tobacco rattle virus-induced gene silencing (VIGS) and particle bombardment approaches.
   - Silencing of **PacCYP707A1** stimulated a slight increase in fruit colouring and enhanced resistance to dehydration stress compared with control fruits.
   - These results suggest that PacCYP707A2 is a key regulator of ABA catabolism that functions as a negative regulator of fruit ripening, while **PacCYP707A1** regulates ABA content in response to dehydration during fruit development.
