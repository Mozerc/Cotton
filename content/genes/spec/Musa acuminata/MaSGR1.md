## Functional Descriptions:
   - RING-type ubiquitin E3 ligase MaRZF1 interacts with chlorophyll catabolic enzyme **MaSGR1**, which promotes the ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation of **MaSGR1**, causing the green ripening of banana fruit under high temperature.
   - These findings unravel an important novel regulatory cascade involving the MaRZF1-**MaSGR1** complex that modulates high temperature-repressed chlorophyll degradation, explaining the green ripening banana phenomenon that occurred under high-temperature conditions, as well as enriching regulatory effects understanding of posttranslational modifications in temperature stress-caused fruit colour quality deterioration.
   - In contrast with the decrease in the **MaSGR1** protein level, MaRZF1 protein level was noticeably increased in the anti-**MaSGR1** antibody immunoprecipitated complex in fruits at 30°C compared with fruits at 20°C. These results suggest that high temperature induced ubiquitination of **MaSGR1** and MaRZF1 may participate in this process.
   - Transiently expressing **MaSGR1** in banana peel showed its positive involvement in promoting chlorophyll degradation under high temperature, thereby weakening green ripening phenotype.
